# Composition And Rhythm

## Source Survey

Sample 8-12 frames. Record:

- Face rectangle and gaze direction
- Mouth and microphone
- Gesture envelope
- Stable negative space
- Background screens or objects that can hold cards
- Lighting changes and crop risks

Do not infer safe zones from one frame.

## Layer Roles

| Layer | Job | Allowed support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A-track speaker | trust, expression, reasoning | subtitle; one compact cue |
| Subtitle | continuous comprehension | speaker/B-roll; small highlight |
| Structured card | comparison, list, mechanism | subtitle if separate |
| Proof | evidence | one instruction label; subtitle |
| B-roll | semantic refresh | subtitle; compact label |
| Thesis | hook or final judgment | minimal metadata |

## Density Budget

For 60-90 seconds:

- Keep clean A-track at least 55%.
- Target high-emphasis time around 25-35%.
- Use 6-9 big emphasis beats.
- Use 3-5 full-screen card moments.
- Use at most 2-3 dense lists.

These are ceilings and review bands, not quotas.

## 9:16 Strategy

- Use full-height speaker as the default.
- Raise subtitles above platform controls.
- Place compact tags in verified upper/side negative space.
- Use full-screen B-roll or cards briefly for numbers, steps, mechanisms, and verdicts.
- Make cards proportionally stronger than on 16:9; a tiny desktop card looks accidental on mobile.
- Return to the speaker after every burst.
- Keep face and central gestures clear; do not stack text through the center column.

## 16:9 Strategy

- Keep A-track dominant.
- Place compact cards in real background space, often a monitor or empty side.
- Use a narrow low-opacity breathing B-roll panel rather than constant full-screen cuts when the speaker is visible.
- Balance a right-side card with a subtle left beat rail or sparse keywords, not another card.
- Reduce panel opacity, shadow, and title size until it supports rather than competes.

## B-Roll Rhythm

Use B-roll on concrete nouns, infrastructure, numbers, comparisons, steps, and proof-adjacent beats.

Preserve A-track for:

- Hook and identity
- Skepticism and trust-building
- Jokes and facial expression
- Complex explanation
- Personal judgment
- Final authority

For a key sentence, use 2-4 short shots only when it contains several distinct visual keywords. Do not hold one low-information stock clip for 4-6 seconds. Do not over-correct into constant cutting.

Generated media is best for abstract mechanisms and infrastructure. Real recordings remain the proof layer. Use each generated or stock clip once by default; repeated clips feel recycled.

## Rhythm Pattern

A useful recurring structure:

1. Impact frame
2. Clean speaker/trust
3. Structure or mechanism
4. Evidence panel
5. Clean speaker/judgment
6. Verdict

Alternate intensity. A video with no quiet A-track feels as flat as a video with no motion.

## Transition Discipline

- Finish one primary layer before the next becomes dominant.
- Return to clean A-track after a burst unless the next sentence has a stronger visual need.
- Use section turns for visual resets.
- Keep the subtitle position stable through track changes.
- Let the final sentence breathe; do not end on generic stock footage.
