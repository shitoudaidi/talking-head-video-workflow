# Cover System

## Goal

Make the topic legible in a feed, preserve the same conflict as the video hook, and avoid poster-like information overload.

## Required Structure

Use only what earns a place:

1. Small brand or series line
2. Optional category label
3. Dominant conflict headline
4. One supporting judgment
5. Optional three short category keywords

Do not use all five by default.

## Headline

- Prefer 3-10 Chinese characters per line.
- Preserve one unanswered question unless the content is a direct tutorial.
- Use a conflict, reversal, cost, mistake, or surprising result.
- Do not summarize the whole video.
- Make the main line readable at phone-feed size.

## Composition

For a person-led cover:

- Choose a frame with clear expression and usable gaze direction.
- Put the headline in the counter-space opposite the face.
- Mask/composite intentionally when needed; do not paste a raw screenshot.
- Keep face edge, hair, and shoulders clean.

For a no-person cover:

- Use one recognizable object, product state, or semantic scene.
- Avoid generic atmospheric AI imagery.
- Keep a quiet title field.

## Typography

- Render all Chinese locally.
- Use the same CJK family and accent semantics as the video.
- Main hook: dominant heavy face, compact line-height, semantic breaks.
- Supporting judgment: visibly smaller and quieter.
- Use orange for the conflict phrase and blue for the mechanism or conclusion.
- Avoid outlines on every word. Use shadow or localized dark field for contrast.

## Generated Backgrounds

Prompt for:

- No text, letters, numbers, subtitles, logos, or watermark
- No people unless the concept requires one
- One clear focal object and one quiet title area
- Actual product/object/state when inspection matters

Reject pseudo-text and fake UI. Generated background is atmosphere or illustration, never proof.

## Review

Check at full size and thumbnail size:

- Can the headline be read in one glance?
- Is the product/topic visually identifiable?
- Does the supporting line sharpen rather than repeat?
- Is there one focal point?
- Does the cover promise the same conflict the video delivers?
- Is every Chinese character deterministic and correct?
